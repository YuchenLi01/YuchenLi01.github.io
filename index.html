The Limitations of Limited Context for Constituency Parsing
Yuchen Li*, Andrej Risteski*,
Association for Computational Linguistics (ACL), 2021   (Oral Presentation)
bibtex / My Twitter post / Andrej's Twitter post

How well can you parse a sentence from left to right in one pass? We theoretically quantify how the "context" affects the representational power of parsing models.

More specifically, we show that, the context for the calculation at each word must be unbounded & in both directions (left & right), in order to have full parsing power, while even if the context is bounded in one direction, the parsing power is quite limited for some hard grammars.

The results were proven for various leading approaches to constituency parsing, but the method is applicable to other models, too.

Complexity of Leading Digit Sequences
Xinwei He*, AJ Hildebrand*, Yuchen Li*, Yunyi Zhang*,
Journal of Discrete Mathematics & Theoretical Computer Science (DMTCS), vol. 22 no. 1, Automata, Logic and Semantics 2020  
bibtex / poster

Consider the sequence of leading digits of 2^n: 1, 2, 4, 8, 1, 3, 6, ...... What is the complexity of such sequences?

We show that, the block complexity is linear for the sequence of leading digits of a^n in base b (for all square-free integer b>=5 and rational number a>0 that are not integral powers of b). In fact, we give explicit formula for the linear coefficients in terms of a and b, in the above case.

Furthermore, we apply this result to obtain sharp upper and lower bounds and determine the asymptotic behavior for the block complexity.
